This page is a biography dedicated to the man, the myth, the legend, one Justin S. Anderson.

The story:

Justin comes from humble beginnings on the island of Oahu, Hawaii. It is there that he gained his love for lounging, lazing, and procrastination. He lived with his father, who worked as a handy-man doing odd-jobs to put food on the table, and his sister, Lindsay. His free-spirited sister, two years his junior, was his accomplice-in-crime adventuring around the neighborhoods of Kawaii Hai and, later, Aiea. These two rambunctious youth lived out their childhoods in daily juxtaposition, experiencing the beautiful island life, carefree, while enduring the trials and hardships of poverty. Their true escape came in the form of a one-way ticket, out of the quicksand of rising property values and island crowding, to the cheap suburbs of Spokane, Washington.

It is there, in the land of pines and rainy weather, that Linsday and Justin vowed to climb out of the throes of mediocrity and seek out advanced educations. Justin’s path led him to the football fandom Mecca of Washington State University, and Lindsay’s to the hometown comfort and tranquility of Eastern Washington University. It was there that Justin decided his best path towards becoming an educated and productive member of society would be through military service. An attraction to the culture and comparatively higher standards led him to join the Air Force through the Reserve Officers Training Course offered as a cross-town partnership between WSU and the University of Idaho.

After four years of new friendships, new experiences, and new knowledge, Justin was ready to enter active duty in the US Air Force as a 2nd Lieutenant. The first obstacle he faced on his way to becoming a Cyberspace Operations officer was a greuling, merciless technical training school dubbed Undergraduate Cyber Training. He entered the arena with 12 excellent classmates, each driven to establish a new domain in which America would be the leader in the emerging cyber battlefield. During this six month endeavour he was faced with ten hour days in a windowless building learning the dark arts of IT. Along the way he lost teammates, some to challenging courses meant to weed out the History majors, and others to lapses in attention or diligence. A failed block of Undergraduate Cyber Training had big impacts on a young officer’s career. After a failure, the officer had three different options:

  1. Buckle down, study, pass, and graduate after your class rank has plummetted
  2. Study and pass, just to fail in a future block
  3. Don't study and fail the same block again

If options ‘B.’ or ‘C.’ occurred, then the officer faced re-training into a different career field, or possibly even discharge from the Air Force.

Despite the struggles, the support of his then girlfriend and future wife, Colleen, and copious amounts of caffeine, helped guide him to victory over the Air Force’s premier school. He emerged top of the class with Distinguished Graduate accolades and an assignment to the bustling ecosystem of Ramstein Air Base, Germany. It is there he still resides, squandering the money better spent towards retirement, instead for trips to the birthplaces of cultures he can’t fully comprehend with experiences he struggles to remember. So if you, my dear reader, find yourself in the chill of a German morning, be sure to hold out an IPA and say the magic letters, “LT”, and he will come running to meet you.
